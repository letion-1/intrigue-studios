Data and Privacy Policy

At Intrigue Studios, we value your privacy and are committed to protecting your personal data in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and applicable German data protection laws.

1. Information we collect

We may collect the following information through our website, forms, or communication channels:

  • Name
  • Email address
  • Phone number
  • Company information
  • Booking details
  • Website usage data
  • IP address and browser information

Information is collected only when voluntarily submitted or automatically processed through website functionality.

2. Purpose of data processing

We process personal data for purposes including: responding to inquiries, providing requested services, scheduling consultations or bookings, improving website functionality, marketing and communication, analytics and performance tracking.

3. Legal basis for processing

Data processing is carried out in accordance with Article 6 GDPR, including: consent, contractual necessity, legitimate business interests, legal obligations.

4. Cookies and tracking technologies

Our website may use cookies and similar technologies to improve user experience, analyze website traffic, and support advertising and remarketing campaigns. Where legally required, users will be asked for consent before non-essential cookies are activated.

5. Third-party services

We may use third-party providers including Google Analytics, Meta/Facebook Pixel, Calendly, hosting providers, AI and automation platforms, and CRM systems. These providers may process data in accordance with their own privacy policies.

6. Data storage and security

We implement reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect personal data against unauthorized access, disclosure, or misuse. However, no internet transmission can be guaranteed to be completely secure.

7. User rights under GDPR

Users have the right to access their data, correct inaccurate data, request deletion of data, restrict processing, withdraw consent, request data portability, and lodge complaints with a supervisory authority.
